## Do this, not that

_Five pairs. Each pair is a specific, actionable contrast, not a vague
preference._

1. **Do:** Invite plainly ("Whoever you are, you are welcome here.") / **Not:** Market the church ("Experience a life-changing worship encounter.")
2. **Do:** Give the practical facts early (time, place, parking, what to expect) / **Not:** Bury the logistics under mission language.
3. **Do:** Welcome newcomers and doubt out loud / **Not:** Assume insider knowledge or lean on churchy jargon.
4. **Do:** Be concrete about THIS place (name the rooms, the door, the organ) / **Not:** Reach for generic "historic beauty."
5. **Do:** Lead with people and welcome / **Not:** Make the building the hero of every page.

(Pairs 1-5 above are the reference build's and are good defaults; replace any
that don't fit this church.)

## Banned vocabulary

These words are banned from site copy. When writing or editing, flag any of
these and replace them with plain language.

### Generic AI-tells (banned by default across all projects)

- delve
- leverage
- robust
- seamless
- elevate
- tapestry
- realm
- landscape
- testament to
- ever-evolving
- crucial
- pivotal
- meticulous
- navigate (as a verb for non-navigation contexts)
- transformative
- curated experience
- tailored solutions

### Church-copy bans (defaults; adjust per client)

- "do life together"
- "unchurched"
- "worship experience"
- "life-changing" (as hype)
- "passionate about" / "on fire for"
- "radical" (as a hype word)
- "authentic community" (as filler)
- "join us as we..." (church-bulletin filler)

Note: "sanctuary" is fine and encouraged when it means the actual room. Do not
use it as a generic metaphor for "a peaceful space."

---

## Punctuation rule

No em-dashes in site copy: the text visitors read on the live site, including
page copy, component text, and Sanity-authored content. If a sentence needs an
em-dash to work, restructure it, split it into two sentences, or use a colon.

This rule is scoped to site copy only. Code comments, commit messages, plans,
specs, and internal docs may use em-dashes.

---

## Stop when you are done

End the paragraph. Do not add a closing sentence that restates the point. Do
not end with "Feel free to reach out" or any variation of it. The last word of
a section should be the last word that earns its place.
